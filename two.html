Sustainable Brands

Sustainable Denim Brands Versus Fast Fashion Denim Brands

A sustainable denim brand is a brand that incorporates measures throughout its production chain to reduce its environmental footprint.

In an opposing light, a fast fashion denim brand is a brand that mass produces clothing to keep up with current trends and does not prioritize its environmental footprint.

Key Differences between Sustainable and Fast Denim Brands

Sustainable
Fast Fashion
Provide healthy work environments for employees Provide poor work environments for employees
Reduces waste from producing higher quality clothing allowing for longer wear time Increases waste from reduced wear time from producing lower quality trendy clothing
Provide employees with sufficient wages for living To maintain low prices, fast fashion companies provide workers with low wages
Reduced material usage from recycling and reusing materials when possible Higher carbon emissions and material use from large production of clothing

Levi's

Levi’s is a brand commonly associated with denim. The company is commonly associated with denim because of their wide variety of denim products with various styles and fits to satisfy any denim lover. Levi’s attains sustainability through using more sustainable materials and reducing the amount of water used in their denim production. Levi’s utilizes recycled materials wherever possible, rain-fed cottonized hemp, and produces quality products to extend the lifespan of each piece and reduce the need for others.

Levi’s provides a Tailor Shop where individuals can speak with an expert about their clothing, which they can upcycle pieces, customize them or properly recycle them. In addition to selling new denim products, Levi’s has a second-hand shop, which allows consumers to repurpose used denim items to extend their lifespan and reduce the amount of waste from the production of new products.

Nudie Jeans

Nudie Jeans is a sustainable brand that produces a wide range of denim products. The company was founded with the vision of being an all-organic denim brand. This vision was executed through all the Nudie Jeans denim being produced with 100% organic cotton. The company produces quality denim products that obtain an extended lifespan to reduce the need for purchasing and producing more denim items. Nudie Jeans tackles water consumption and contamination in denim production by using no additional treatment or washes.

A unique aspect of Nudie Jean's denim production process involves the use of electricity as opposed to chemicals to solve indigo dye in water and reduce the likelihood of chemical contamination in surrounding areas. Nudie Jeans contains several product lines produced using a large portion of recycled fibers and material. Nudie Jean's commitment to sustainability stretches beyond their products through their choice to only work with other brands that are sustainable with ethical values and provide free lifetime repairs.

Outland Denim

Outland Denim is a brand created in 2011 in Australia, which provides a unique collection of sustainable denim. The brand obtains a vision of producing quality products to extend their lifecycle and reduce the need for different denim products.

Outland Denim chose to invest in their own Wash House and Cut, Make, Trim facilities with technology to minimize their impact on the environment. Outland produces their products with organic cotton that uses zero agrochemicals, which are known to cause water pollution and soil degradation. In addition, organic cotton uses less groundwater and surface water than conventional cotton to help reduce the amount of water used in the denim-making process.
