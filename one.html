Overview of Sustainable Denim

What is Sustainable Denim?

Sustainable denim involves denim that has been produced with the accountability of the environmental issues to reduce the amount of environmental impact from denim production and preserve natural resources.

You might question where sustainable denim comes from? Well, sustainable denim strives from the sustainable fashion movement with a specific focus on the production and use of denim. The sustainable fashion movement targets four different key issues, which include waste creation, chemical usage, energy emissions, and water consumption and contamination.

Sustainable denim tackles waste creation by focusing on reducing the amount of clothing and materials that are sent to landfills or disposed of. Sustainable denim focuses on reducing and eliminating the use of pesticides and chemical for material production to help battle the issue of chemical usage. Sustainable denim challenges energy consumption, water consumption, and contamination by using effective drying, ironing, and washing methods for denim products.


Problems with Current Denim Production

The production of denim has several negative impacts on the environment demonstrating the need for sustainable denim in the current fashion market.

For example,

  • Genetically modified cotton is heavily used in the production of denim. Cotton production tends to use large amounts of water.

  • Cotton production involves the use of various insecticides and pesticides, which pose health risks to the local ecosystem and organisms.

  • Chemicals, including toxic substances, used to dye cotton fibers can be dumped into the waterways of developing countries producing denim.

  • Subprocesses that make denim softer and lighter colors involve the use of vast amounts of water.

  • Denim can be composed using synthetic fibers that cannot be decomposed or recycled resulting in an increased amount of waste in landfills.

“Around 20 per cent of global industrial water pollution is from dyeing and textile treatment.” – United Nations


Technologies Used in Sustainable Denim

Technology Description
NoStone NoStone involves the use of abrasive stainless-steel drums in washing machines to create different levels of abrasion to help reduce the use of pumice stones. NoStone helps to reduce emissions, waste, and water consumption.
Ozone Ozone is a bleaching agent that requires fewer rinses than traditional bleaching methods and reduces the amount of water used. Following bleaching, remaining ozone can be safely converted into water and oxygen, which reduces the number of chemicals entering waterways and ecosystems.
Enzymes Enzymes, such as laccases, are combined with oxidation processes to help alter the indigo dye in the bleaching process, which serves as an alternative to harmful chemicals that can enter waterways and ecosystems.
Laser Lasers are used to produce a variety of effects on denim, which include intentional tears and holes, patterns, patches, and vintage effects. Lasers replace the need for Potassium Permanganate, a harmful chemical that negatively impacts workers because it attributes to short and long-term health effects.
